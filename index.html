Background

My Story

I didn't start in analytics — I started where the data lives: on the front lines. For over five years, I worked in technical support, sitting at the intersection of customer problems, system behavior, and operational gaps. I answered the questions no dashboard could anticipate, and I learned to see patterns in the chaos.

Over time, I realized the most impactful thing I could do wasn't just solve individual problems — it was understand why those problems kept happening. That curiosity pulled me toward analysis. I started asking better questions, building reports, and finding ways to surface insights that teams could actually use.

What I love most is bringing insights to life visually — building dashboards that make complex data immediately clear, and turning scattered numbers into a narrative someone can act on. There's something deeply satisfying about watching a well-built visualization change the way a team thinks about a problem.

Today I combine that real-world operational grounding with technical skills in SQL, Python, Tableau, and Excel to deliver analysis that is both rigorous and practical. I know what it looks like when processes break, and I know how to use data to fix them.
